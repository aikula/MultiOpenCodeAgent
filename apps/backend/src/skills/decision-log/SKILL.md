# Decision Log

Use this skill to capture a decision in a structured format that creates a clear audit trail and prevents revisiting settled questions.

## When to use

- A team has reached a decision and needs to document it.
- A manager wants a record of what was decided and why.
- You need to log a sequence of decisions for a project retrospective.

## Input

Provide the decision topic, the chosen option, alternatives considered, and any relevant context or discussion notes.

## Output format

1. **Decision title** - Short, descriptive label.
2. **Date** - When the decision was made.
3. **Status** - Proposed, accepted, or superseded.
4. **Context** - Two to three sentences explaining why the decision was needed.
5. **Options considered** - Brief list with pros and cons for each.
6. **Decision** - The option chosen and a one-sentence rationale.
7. **Owner** - Person accountable for follow-through.
8. **Reversibility** - Easy, moderate, or hard to reverse, with reasoning.

## Guidelines

- Be explicit about what triggered the decision.
- Record the rationale even when it seems obvious; context fades quickly.
- If the decision supersedes a prior one, reference the earlier entry.
