# Email Draft

Use this skill to compose or refine professional emails so they are clear, concise, and appropriately toned.

## When to use

- A manager asks you to draft an email from bullet points or a rough idea.
- You need to rewrite a draft to improve clarity or adjust tone.
- You want to reply to an incoming message in a professional manner.

## Input

Provide the purpose of the email, the recipient, key points to convey, and the desired tone (formal, neutral, friendly). Include any prior email thread if this is a reply.

## Output format

1. **Subject line** - Clear, specific, under 10 words.
2. **Body** - Two to four short paragraphs with a logical flow.
3. **Call to action** - One sentence stating what you need from the recipient and by when.

## Guidelines

- Open with context; do not assume the recipient remembers prior exchanges.
- Use short paragraphs and bullet lists for readability.
- Avoid passive-aggressive language; if the situation is tense, be direct but respectful.
- Do not include filler phrases like "I hope this email finds you well" unless the user requests it.
- Proofread for tone consistency before returning the draft.
