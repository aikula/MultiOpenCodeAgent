# Meeting Brief

Use this skill to turn scattered meeting notes or transcripts into a clear, structured brief that participants can review before or after the meeting.

## When to use

- You have raw notes from a meeting and need to organize them.
- You need to prepare a pre-meeting brief from agenda items and context.
- A manager asks you to summarize what happened in a call.

## Input

Provide the raw notes, agenda, or transcript. Optionally include the meeting title, date, and attendee list.

## Output format

1. **Meeting overview** - Title, date, purpose, and key participants.
2. **Discussion points** - Numbered list of major topics with a one-sentence summary each.
3. **Decisions made** - Bullet list of confirmed decisions with owners.
4. **Action items** - Table with task, owner, and deadline.
5. **Open items** - Unresolved questions or topics tabled for follow-up.

## Guidelines

- Keep each discussion point to one or two sentences.
- Distinguish clearly between decisions and opinions.
- If an action item lacks an owner or deadline, flag it explicitly.
- Do not invent information that is missing from the input.
