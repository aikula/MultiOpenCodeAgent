# Web Research

Use this skill to take raw research material from the web and turn it into a concise, structured summary that a manager can act on.

## When to use

- A manager asks for a summary of research on a topic.
- You have gathered multiple web sources and need to synthesize key findings.
- A decision requires market, competitor, or technology research.

## Input

Provide the research topic, the source URLs or raw content, and the specific question the manager needs answered.

## Output format

1. **Research question** - Restate the question the research aims to answer.
2. **Key findings** - Five to seven bullet points, each with a one-sentence takeaway and source reference.
3. **Contradictions** - Any points where sources disagree, noted explicitly.
4. **Confidence level** - High, medium, or low, based on source quality and consistency.
5. **Further reading** - Up to three recommended sources for deeper exploration.

## Guidelines

- Attribute each finding to its source; do not present opinions as facts.
- Flag any sources that may be biased or unreliable.
- Keep the summary focused on the decision at hand, not general background.
- If sources are insufficient to answer the question, say so clearly.
